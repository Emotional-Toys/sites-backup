Hello Aibo Type "B" tips for English users


Hello Aibo Type "B" is a Japanese only stick for the ERS-210 and ERS-220. You must speak to it in phonetic Japanese.

This web page provides help for English speakers to control this Japanese only AiboWare.

My Notes

  • If you have most of the ERS-2x0 personality software, you may want to try this stick for a new experience.
  • Works in any model ERS-2x0/2x0A. Will perform different actions in an ERS-220 than in an ERS-210.
  • This is very different than the "Hello Aibo" (Type A) that came out at the time of AiboLife 2.
  • "Type A" and "Type B" refer to the Japanese interpretation of blood type, not the more American notion of a "Type A" personality. In fact it is somewhat the opposite. Hello Type A (the old Hello AIBO) is calm and collected (like the Nice Guy Adult personality). Hello Type B (this stick) is curious with many interests.
  • This stick has a very large vocabulary, and is only available in Japanese.
  • The new stick comes with a Japanese vocabulary. You must speak to your AIBO using phonetic Japanese.

    To give a voice command to your Japanese AIBO:

  • Find the voice command you want to give in the last column ("english interpretation"). For example "shake paw" (number 3).
  • Do not say the phrase "shake paw" to your AIBO
  • Instead look at the middle columns, and pick one of the phonetic phrases, for example: 'a k u sh y u'. They are spelled out in phonemes as well as the Romanji word.
  • Try pronouncing them based on how you think they will sound (for example: 'ahh koo shoe you').
  • If that doesn't work, try another pronunciation for the same voice command (semid), for example: 'o t e sh i t e' (oh tay she tay).

    TIP: Try to group them together in consonant + vowel pairs.
    TIP: Try pronouncing "/N/" like the letter 'n' or the letter 'm'.
    TIP: If you see strange symbols or numbers, you can ignore them.
    TIP: There is no letter L.

    Good luck.
