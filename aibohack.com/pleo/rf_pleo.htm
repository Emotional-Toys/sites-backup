Radio Control Pleo

Pleo's serial debug terminal (monitor)

Pleo comes with several diagnostic services that let you monitor the behavior of a running program over a serial port. This is not necessary for MySkit/PleoMap or YAPT programming. IMHO it is essential for any Pawn programming and debugging is the ability to see 'printf' style messages in real-time.
You can use Pleo's USB cable to connect to a PC (instructions coming soon). I prefer using the hardware serial port instead. It has several advantages, notaably it is always available (no USB startup problems) and can easily be converted to a wireless signal.

The hardware serial port comes out of the bottom of the robot and it can be used with a voltage shifter You can easily remove the plastic cover next to the on-off switch (NOTE: it will come off easily, but it won't go back on).
So far we don't know the proper part number for the connector. The hardware serial port is low voltage (3.3V) so don't hook it directly to the RS-232 port on your PC. Adapter boards are available (including this USB one.

For simple connection to the robot, you need an RF connection that will run at 115,200 bps. It should be a 3.3V device so it can be powered by the Pleo.
NOTE: the interface to Pleo runs at 115,200bps, but the radio transmitter can't run continuously at that speed. Sometimes data is dropped. This is not important for most text monitor needs.
There are two good candidates:
  • BlueSMiRF device. This is a Bluetooth module. You connect one to your robot. Recommended if your computer already has Bluetooth, otherwise you must buy a Bluetooth<->USB 'dongle' and install the proper software on your PC. Available for purchase at SparkFun.
  • XBee radio modules. This is a proprietary protocol. You will need two of them. Development kits currently on sale at digi.com (the $99 development kit is all you need). Optional mounting parts are available for purchase at SparkFun (one of these and two of these, or solder it directly like I did).

I have successfully used the XBee setup recommended here.

What is it good for

So far the RF connection is good for debugging Pawn programs, or examining Pleo's default personality when it runs.
The serial terminal could be used for other remote control applications.
Unfortunately because of the way Pleo is built, transferring camera video or audio from the microphones over wireless is not practical. The camera and microphones go into the 2nd ARM CPU. The debug serial port is attached to the main CPU.
