ERS-7 Language Voice Commands - Phonetic Japanese

This page describes phonetic Japanese for English speakers who wish to try out the Japanese version of AiboMind.
These voice command work if you have a Japanese version of AiboMind (ERS-7AW01J) or an English stick with the Japanese vocabulary loaded on top of it.

If you are running the English vocabulary, please refer to this page.

To give a voice command to your Japanese AIBO:

  • Find the voice command you want to give in the last column ("equivalent English"). For example "shake hands" (number 62)
  • Do not say the phrase "shake hands" to your AIBO
  • Instead look at the second column, and pick one of the phonetic phrases, for example: 'a k u sh y u'.
  • Try pronouncing them based on how you think they will sound (for example: 'ahh koo shoe you').
  • If that doesn't work, try another pronunciation for the same voice command (semid), for example: 'o t e sh i t e' (oh tay she tay).

    TIP: Try to group them together in consonant + vowel pairs.
    TIP: Try pronouncing "/N/" like the letter 'n' or the letter 'm'.
    TIP: If you see strange symbols or numbers, you can ignore them.
    TIP: There is no letter L.
    Good luck.
