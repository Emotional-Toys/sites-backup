Autopsy Photos

Take off most of the plastic, and this is what you get:

There are three motors (two for locomotion, one for spinning the head). The H-Bridges for controlling the three motors are on the main board.

There are quite a few sensors:
  • Three microphones (one on the body, two in the head)
  • Sonar (tranmitter and receiver) in the head
  • Various LEDs (Red&Blue for mood, and White for 'Laser' in head, 'Stay' LED in body)
  • IR body heat sensor in the head
  • Piezo-electric sensor at top of the head (for detecting when you bonk him on the head)

    There is a 48 pin CPU on the back-side of the main board. It is an epoxied die on a chip carrier board with no markings (the make/manufacturer is unknown). There is also a smaller chip, epoxied to a separate board attached to the front of the main circuit board (hard to see in photos). It is probably the voice recognition chip (since it is right next to the body microphone).

    No expansion port or other ways to customize the software/firmware of the robot (other than ripping out his brain).
