List of Hackable Robots and Robo-Dogs

AIBO (the 'top dog')

Summary
Product Status: Being produced and supported by Sony.
Many models available: ERS-210, 220, 311, 312, 31L (and variants)
Classic models discontinued: ERS-110, ERS-111
Variety of accessories, software, etc from Sony and 3rd parties.
Established leader in entertainment robot field.
Hackability: High (memory stick software, officially supported programming tools)
Cost: High: from $600 for ERS-31L to $1500 for ERS-220"A"
Accessories: Many (batteries, charging stations, different software,
WiFi LAN card, bags, tongues, tails, jewelry, ...)
Hardware hacks: Not recommended (robot is too expensive and already has most of what you need)
Add on WiFi LAN card for ERS-210 and ERS-220 series.
Home brew RS-232 port for low level debugging.
Software hacks: Many (what this site is all about AiboHack.com)
Unique features: Full featured robot dog.
Color camera, voice recognition, complex personality software, optional WiFi connection, ...
Many programming options (RCODE, GUI tools, C++)
Disadvantages: High cost.
Websites: AiboHack.com - This site
aibo.com - main Sony AIBO site (worldwide)
www.us.aibo.com - US AIBO site
www.jp.aibo.com - Japanese AIBO site (good for new products)
AiboSite - BBS and downloads (US)
AiboLife - BBS and Chat (US)
AiboWorld - BBS (UK)

Hackable iCybie

Summary
Product Status: Originally from Tiger/Hasbro, now discontinued.
Price wars lowered $200 retail price to around $50.
Accessories being sold by SilverLit.
Future uncertain.
Hackability: High (plug-in cartridge)
Cost: Low ($50 for robot, more for accessories)
Accessories: NiMH batteries, walkup charger, downloader, colored shells
Hardware hacks: RS-232 port, RF modem, camera, ...
Software hacks: Many (see images page)
Unique features: Low cost robot dog.
Can walk on four legs. Walkup charger.
Voice recognition (8 speaker dependent commands)
Disadvantages: Low cost 'toy' design.
Websites: AiboHack.com/icybie - ICybie and Super ICybie
I-Cybie.com - for online manuals and ordering accessories
SilverLit.com - for downloader program and personalities
ICybie BBS on Yahoo Groups


R2D2

Summary
Product Status: Recently introduced from Hasbro.
Hackability: Low (sealed self contained toy)
Cost: Moderate ($100 retail)
Accessories: None (other than a beer can)
Hardware hacks: Click here for autopsy photos
Not easy to extend functionality (closed design)
Good to salvage for parts after price comes down.
Software hacks: n/a
Unique features: Many speaker independent voice commands.
Star Wars character.
Disadvantages: Closed design.
Websites: R2DroidFactory
CreatureLabs site (creator)

Hackable WonderBorg

--
Summary
Product Status: Originally from Bandai in Japan.
Imported to the US by Hasbro/Tiger.
Hackability: Medium (PC link, but closed firmware)
Cost: Relatively Low ($60 if on sale)
Accessories: None (there is a WonderSwan version sold in Japan)
Hardware hacks: It is a robot kit.
There are building options (different wheels, antennae etc)
Expansion connector (for an extra motor and sensor)
Software hacks: Programmable with GUI tool and IR download
Similar to Lego Mindstorms
No known software 'hacks'.
Unique features: Low cost legged robot kit.
Simple programmability with PC link.
Disadvantages: Six legs driven by two motors (ie. not that much leg control)
Limited programmability.
Websites: Main site (Roboken) - Japanese.
Main site - partial English

Mousies

Summary
Product Status: Originally from Tiger Toys.
Discontinued (I think) - hard to find.
Hackability: Low (sealed toy)
Cost: Low (if you can find them)
Accessories: None (comes with a ball)
Hardware hacks: Difficult.
Software hacks: Simple "downloading" of enabling codes.
Unique features: Small wheeled robot.
Great at ball tracking.
Downloads feature enabling codes by flashes on PC screen.
Disadvantages: Closed design.
Websites: Tiger website
Mousies Flash page
